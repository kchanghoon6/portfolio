Structural Health

Korea Code Fair 2026 · Flagship

SENTRY — Low-Cost Bridge Anomaly Detection

Continuous vibration screening for small bridges, robust to temperature & humidity.

A low-cost MEMS-accelerometer system that flags abnormal bridge vibration. It learns how weather shifts a healthy bridge's natural frequency and compensates for it, then an autoencoder detects anomalies in the residual — with explainable, band-wise alarms that prioritize inspection. Validated on the public Z24 bridge benchmark and a custom bridge model.

  • PyTorch
  • Autoencoder
  • Signal Processing
  • Raspberry Pi

A/B Testing

A/B Deep #1 — Heterogeneous Treatment Effects

Finding which segments actually respond to a feature.

An honest causal tree that discovers high-lift user segments from an experiment. It learns the splits and estimates effects on separate data ("honest"), extracts the top segments, then re-validates them on a holdout with a shrinkage check to expose false discoveries.

  • Causal Inference
  • Honest Trees
  • NumPy

A/B Testing

A/B Deep #2 — Hierarchical-Bayes Shrinkage

Reading 40 simultaneous experiments without fooling yourself.

A hierarchical-Bayesian meta-analysis of 40 concurrent A/B tests. A Normal–Normal empirical-Bayes model shrinks noisy per-experiment estimates toward the global mean (borrowing strength, à la James–Stein), then ranks experiments by P(ROI > 0) with an exploration bonus.

  • Bayesian
  • Empirical Bayes
  • Shrinkage

A/B Testing

A/B Deep #4 — Network Interference

Measuring effects when users influence each other.

Estimating treatment effects when SUTVA breaks on a social graph. On a Barabási–Albert network it separates the direct effect from spillover (and their interaction) via exposure mapping and logistic regression, with block-bootstrap CIs — showing how a naive A/B is biased.

  • Causal Inference
  • Networks
  • Bootstrap

A/B Testing

A/B Deep #5 — Switchback & HAC Errors

Trustworthy inference on a time-randomized experiment.

Analyzing a marketplace switchback experiment (time-unit ABBA randomization). Because autocorrelation makes naive OLS standard errors overconfident, it corrects them with Newey–West (HAC) errors and adds winsorize / Huber robustness for promotion spikes.

  • Time Series
  • HAC / Newey–West
  • Robust Stats

About

A bit about me

I'm a high-school student at CheongShim International Academy, building at the intersection of machine learning, statistics, and software. What I care about most is the part that's easy to skip: making something that actually holds up — validated, honestly evaluated, and useful to a real person, not just a number in a notebook.

My projects tend to go all the way through. For the Korea Code Fair I built SENTRY, a low-cost sensor system that screens small bridges for abnormal vibration using weather-compensated signals and an autoencoder. For the Wharton high-school data-science competition I led a team on hockey analytics, and I keep working through hard experimentation problems — honest causal trees, hierarchical-Bayes shrinkage, network interference, switchback designs — because careful inference is where the interesting parts hide.

I also like building things people actually use: I founded ywc.kr, an opportunity-finding platform my classmates rely on, and built my school's student-court site end to end. I tutor computer science on Schoolhouse.world, keep my fundamentals sharp with competitive programming (USACO Silver), and treat clear documentation and reproducibility as part of the work — not an afterthought.
